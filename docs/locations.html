Location Collection

Three leading landscapes.

These case studies translate the model from scores into places. Each page combines the national screening output with a more specific landscape reading, so visitors can see not just where a zone appears, but what kind of landscape it represents today and why the model finds it promising.

How to read this collection

The three featured locations are not “the answer” to where rewilding should happen. They are strong examples of what the model is actually finding: a moorland-edge mosaic in Cornwall, a peat-based wetland landscape in Somerset, and a northern borderland zone shaped by lowland peat restoration context. Together they show that the workflow is surfacing different restoration geographies rather than repeating one landscape type everywhere.

Why this page matters

A location collection makes the project easier to understand publicly. Instead of asking people to jump straight from methods to raw rankings, it offers a middle layer: concrete landscapes, interpretable metrics, and a careful explanation of what the model suggests and what still requires local evidence.

The locations page is a guided route into the stronger public examples from the current canonical run.
